Aisa Custodio (PhD Student)

"I completed my undergraduate and master's degrees in Materials Engineering at the University of the Philippines-Diliman. I caught the “coating fever” during my time at an international aerospace company, where I contributed to the production of precision flight control components for commercial aircraft. Here, I gained experience in high velocity oxygen fuel (HVOF) processing of WC and T400 coatings. Presently, I am pursuing my PhD in Mechanical Engineering at the University of New Brunswick, conducting research in the Alloy Design and Materials Testing Research Laboratory, and Nanocomposites and Mechanics Laboratory. My focus lies in the development of high entropy alloy feedstock through mechanical alloying and its application in cold spray - making materials that can do extraordinary things! Outside my academic pursuits, I find joy in running and hiking adventures."
